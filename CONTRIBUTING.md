# Contributing to Persistra RFC Series (PCS)

---

## Change Control

**Change Control:** Exocortical Concepts, Inc.

The Persistra RFC Series (PCS) is maintained and controlled by Exocortical Concepts, Inc.

All changes to PCS specifications are subject to approval by Exocortical Concepts, Inc.

---

## External Feedback

External feedback is welcome and encouraged.

### How to Provide Feedback

1. **Open a GitHub Issue** in this repository
2. **Describe the issue or suggestion** clearly and concisely
3. **Reference specific RFC sections** where applicable
4. **Provide rationale** for proposed changes

### Review Process

- All issues will be reviewed by Exocortical Concepts, Inc.
- Acceptance of proposed changes is at the **sole discretion** of Exocortical Concepts, Inc.
- Feedback may be incorporated, modified, or declined without obligation

---

## What We Welcome

- **Clarification requests** on ambiguous specification language
- **Technical corrections** (typos, broken links, formatting issues)
- **Implementation experience reports** from PCS adopters
- **Interoperability concerns** between PCS implementations
- **Security considerations** not addressed in current specifications

---

## What We Do Not Accept

- **Pull requests** modifying specification content (issues only)
- **Demands** for specific changes or features
- **Proprietary implementation details** that should remain private
- **Marketing or promotional content**

---

## Intellectual Property

By submitting feedback, you acknowledge that:

- Your contribution may be incorporated into PCS specifications
- You grant Exocortical Concepts, Inc. the right to use your feedback
- You do not claim ownership of PCS specifications or derivative works
- You do not receive patent licenses or certification rights

---

## Governance

PCS does not currently have a formal governance council or standards body.

Change control remains with Exocortical Concepts, Inc. until such time as a governance structure is established.

---

## Versioning

PCS follows semantic versioning for specifications:

- **Major versions** (e.g., 1.x.x → 2.x.x) — Breaking changes to normative requirements
- **Minor versions** (e.g., 1.0.x → 1.1.x) — Backward-compatible additions
- **Patch versions** (e.g., 1.0.0 → 1.0.1) — Clarifications, typos, non-normative changes
